\begin{abstract}
  This entry formalizes a prime-generated version of Nagata's factoriality
  theorem in Isabelle/HOL.  It develops the basic theory of prime-generated
  multiplicative sets, packages a wrapper interface around the AFP entry
  \texttt{Localization\_Ring}, and proves record-based descent theorems showing
  that factoriality descends from a localization to the base ring under
  prime-generated and prime-or-unit hypotheses on the multiplicative set.  The
  theorem package also includes closure-based corollaries for arbitrary and
  finite families of prime generators.  The application layer specializes this
  framework to polynomial rings, both for localization away from the
  polynomial variable \(X\) and for localizations generated by constant
  prime polynomials.
\end{abstract}

\section{Overview}

This entry formalizes a prime-generated version of Nagata's factoriality
theorem for noetherian domains, following the classical commutative-algebra
framework of Nagata~\cite{Nagata1962}, Samuel~\cite{Samuel1964}, and
Matsumura~\cite{Matsumura1986}.
The central result states that if \(R\) is a noetherian domain and \(S\) is a
prime-generated multiplicative submonoid of \(R\) such that the localization
\(S^{-1}R\) is a UFD, then \(R\) itself is a UFD.  A multiplicative set is
\emph{prime-generated} when every element is a finite product of elements
that are both prime in \(R\) and members of \(S\).

The formalization is organized in six theories.
\texttt{Prime\_Generated} develops the combinatorial theory of prime-generated
multiplicative sets independently of any localization.
\texttt{Localization\_Interface} provides a wrapper layer over the AFP entry
\texttt{Localization\_Ring} with lemmas for representative equality,
cross-multiplication, units, and injectivity of the canonical map.
\texttt{Nagata\_Lemmas} contains the record-based descent proofs, including
both the prime-generated and the prime-or-unit variants, together with
closure-based corollaries for arbitrary and finite families of prime generators.
\texttt{Polynomial\_Applications} and \texttt{Fraction\_Field\_Applications}
specialize the framework to polynomial rings, for localization away from the
variable~\(X\) and for localizations generated by constant prime polynomials,
respectively.  \texttt{Nagata\_Factoriality} ties the entry together as the
top-level theory.
